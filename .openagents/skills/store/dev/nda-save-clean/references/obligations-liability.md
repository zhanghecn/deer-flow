# Obligations and Liability Analysis

## Receiving Party Obligations

### Standard Obligations

1. **Use Restriction**: Use information only for the stated purpose
2. **Safeguarding Duty**: Protect with "reasonable care" or "same degree of care as own confidential info"
3. **Disclosure Limitation**: Only disclose to employees/agents with need-to-know
4. **Return/Destruction**: Return or destroy upon termination

### Risk Assessment

| Obligation Type | Standard Language | Risk if Missing/Misused |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------------|
| Use restriction | "Solely for the purpose of evaluating the potential business relationship" | 🔴 High - Unrestricted use |
| Safeguarding | "At least the same degree of care as own confidential information" | 🟡 Medium - Vague standard |
| Need-to-know | "Only to employees with a need to know" | 🟡 Medium - Broad disclosure risk |
| Return/destruction | "Return or destroy within 30 days of termination" | 🟡 Medium - Data retention risk |

### Red Flags in Obligations

| Issue | Severity | Concern |
|-------|----------|---------|
| No stated purpose limitation | 🔴 High | Information can be used for any purpose |
| "Highest degree of care" standard | 🟡 Medium | Unrealistic standard |
| Disclosure to "affiliates" without restriction | 🟡 Medium | Broad sharing without control |
| No return/destruction obligation | 🟡 Medium | Information remains indefinitely |

## Liability Provisions

### Indemnification

**Typical Issues:**
- One-sided indemnification (only Receiving Party indemnifies)
- Unlimited indemnification scope
- Indemnification for third-party claims without defense obligation

**Standard Pattern:**
> "The Receiving Party shall indemnify the Disclosing Party against losses arising from breach of this Agreement."

**Red Flag:** Unlimited indemnification without cap

### Limitation of Liability

**What to Check:**
- Is there a liability cap?
- Are consequential damages excluded?
- Is the exclusion mutual?

**Standard Exclusion:**
> "In no event shall either party be liable for indirect, incidental, special, or consequential damages."

**Red Flags:**

| Issue | Severity | Concern |
|-------|----------|---------|
| No liability cap | 🔴 High | Unlimited exposure |
| One-sided exclusion | 🟡 Medium | Unfair allocation |
| No exclusion of consequential damages | 🟡 Medium | Potential for large claims |

### Liquidated Damages

**Valid Use:** Reasonable estimate of actual damages
**Invalid Use:** Penalty clause (may be unenforceable)

**Red Flag:** Excessive liquidated damages that appear punitive

## Remedy Provisions

### Injunction Rights

**Standard Language:**
> "The Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief to prevent breach or threatened breach, without posting bond."

**Risk Assessment:**

| Provision | Standard? | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Injunction available | ✅ Yes | Common and reasonable |
| No bond requirement | 🟡 Negotiable | Benefits discloser |
| Exclusive remedy | 🔴 High Risk | Limits other remedies |

### Breach Consequences

**Typical Provisions:**
- Right to terminate agreement
- Return of confidential information
- Injunctive relief
- Monetary damages

**Red Flag:** "Exclusive remedy" clause that limits all other remedies

## Recommended Redlines

### For Unlimited Liability

**Original:** "The Receiving Party shall be liable for all damages arising from any breach."

**Redline:** "The Receiving Party's total liability under this Agreement shall not exceed [Amount/Confidential Information Value]."

### For One-Sided Indemnification

**Original:** "Recipient shall indemnify Discloser for all claims..."

**Redline:** "Each party shall indemnify the other for claims arising from its breach of this Agreement."

### For No Purpose Limitation

**Original:** "Recipient may use the information for any business purpose."

**Redline:** "Recipient shall use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of [specific purpose]."
