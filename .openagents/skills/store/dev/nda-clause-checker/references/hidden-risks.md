# Hidden Risks and Red Flags

## Overview

Hidden risks are clauses that appear standard but contain provisions that can significantly impact business operations beyond confidentiality. These are the most dangerous clauses because they are often overlooked during NDA review.

## Non-Compete Clauses

### Detection

Look for language restricting:
- Competitive business activities
- Working with competitors
- Developing similar products/services

### Typical Hidden Language

> "Receiving Party agrees not to develop, market, or sell products that compete with Disclosing Party's products for a period of [X] years."

> "Recipient shall not engage in any business that is competitive with Discloser's business."

### Risk Assessment

| Factor | Severity |
|--------|----------|
| Explicit non-compete in NDA | 🔴 High |
| Broad definition of "competitive" | 🔴 High |
| Duration > 1 year | 🔴 High |
| No geographic limitation | 🔴 High |

### Why It's Dangerous

- NDAs are meant to protect information, not prevent competition
- Non-competes should be separate agreements with proper consideration
- May be unenforceable in some jurisdictions (e.g., California)
- Can block legitimate business opportunities

### Recommended Redline

**Original:** "Recipient shall not, for a period of two years, engage in any business competitive with Discloser."

**Redline:** DELETE ENTIRE CLAUSE. Alternatively: "This Agreement does not restrict Recipient from competing with Discloser, provided Recipient does not use Discloser's Confidential Information."

## IP Assignment Clauses

### Detection

Look for language about:
- Ownership of derivative works
- Assignment of improvements
- Rights to inventions made during term

### Typical Hidden Language

> "All improvements, derivatives, or modifications of Confidential Information shall be owned by Disclosing Party."

> "Any inventions conceived during the term of this Agreement relating to Discloser's business shall be assigned to Discloser."

### Risk Assessment

| Provision | Severity |
|-----------|----------|
| Derivative works assignment | 🔴 High |
| Invention assignment | 🔴 High |
| "Relating to business" without limitation | 🔴 High |
| No exception for pre-existing IP | 🔴 High |

### Why It's Dangerous

- Could claim ownership of your independent work
- Vague "relating to business" language is overbroad
- May inadvertently assign valuable IP

### Recommended Redline

**Original:** "All improvements based on Confidential Information shall belong to Discloser."

**Redline:** "For clarity, this Agreement does not transfer any intellectual property rights. Recipient retains all rights to its pre-existing IP and independently developed improvements."

## Non-Solicitation Clauses

### Detection

Look for restrictions on:
- Hiring Discloser's employees
- Soliciting Discloser's customers
- Doing business with Discloser's vendors

### Typical Hidden Language

> "Recipient shall not hire or solicit any of Discloser's employees for a period of [X] years."

> "Recipient shall not solicit any customers of Discloser with whom Recipient had contact during this Agreement."

### Risk Assessment

| Factor | Severity |
|--------|----------|
| Employee non-solicitation | 🟡 Medium |
| Customer non-solicitation | 🟡 Medium |
| Duration > 1 year | 🟡 Medium |
| No geographic scope | 🟡 Medium |

### Why It's Dangerous

- Restricts hiring and business development
- Should be separate agreement with consideration
- May conflict with employment mobility laws

### Recommended Redline

**Original:** "Recipient shall not solicit Discloser's employees for one year."

**Redline:** DELETE or limit to: "Recipient shall not actively solicit Discloser's employees with whom Recipient had direct contact during this Agreement, for six months after termination."

## Jurisdiction and Venue

### Detection

Look for:
- Governing law clause
- Venue/jurisdiction clause
- Arbitration requirement

### Typical Problematic Language

> "This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of [Discloser's State], and any disputes shall be resolved in the courts of [Discloser's City]."

### Risk Assessment

| Situation | Severity |
|-----------|----------|
| Opposing party's home jurisdiction | 🟡 Medium |
| Foreign jurisdiction | 🔴 High |
| Mandatory arbitration | 🟡 Medium |
| Exclusive venue | 🟡 Medium |

### Why It's Dangerous

- Litigation in distant forum is expensive
- Foreign law may be unfavorable
- May waive important rights

### Recommended Redline

**Original:** "Disputes shall be resolved in the courts of New York."

**Redline:** "Disputes shall be resolved in the courts of [Neutral Location] or the state where Defendant resides."

## Reverse Confidentiality Provisions

### Detection

Look for:
- Discloser's obligations to keep Recipient's info confidential
- Mutual protection language
- Asymmetric marking requirements

### Why It's Important

Even in unilateral NDAs, you may share your own information during discussions. Without protection, that information is not covered.

### Recommended Addition

Add if not present:
> "If Recipient discloses its confidential information to Discloser during discussions, Discloser agrees to maintain such information in confidence under terms substantially similar to this Agreement."

## Other Hidden Clauses to Watch

### No-Sublicense Clause

> "Recipient may not sublicense its rights under this Agreement."

**Impact:** Limits flexibility in corporate structures

### Audit Rights

> "Discloser may audit Recipient's compliance with this Agreement."

**Impact:** Creates operational burden and potential exposure

### No-Warranty Clause

> "Discloser makes no warranty regarding accuracy of Confidential Information."

**Standard:** Usually acceptable, but note reliance limitations

### Non-Disparagement

> "Recipient shall not disparage Discloser."

**Impact:** Limits free speech; scope often vague

## Hidden Risks Checklist

Before signing, verify:

- [ ] No non-compete clause embedded
- [ ] No IP assignment overreach
- [ ] No employee/customer non-solicitation
- [ ] Jurisdiction is reasonable or neutral
- [ ] Audit rights are limited or absent
- [ ] No reverse confidentiality gap (if you may share info)
- [ ] No non-disparagement clause
- [ ] Arbitration clause is fair (if present)
