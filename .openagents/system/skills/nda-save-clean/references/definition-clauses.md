# Definition Clauses Analysis

## Confidential Information Definition

### What to Look For

**Scope of Definition**
- Is the definition too broad? (e.g., "all information shared")
- Is it specific enough? (e.g., "technical data, financial information, customer lists")
- Does it require marking/stamping for confidentiality?
- Does it include oral disclosures with written confirmation requirement?

### Risk Assessment

| Issue | Severity | Why It Matters |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| Overly broad definition ("all information") | 🟡 Medium | Creates unreasonable burden, may be unenforceable |
| Marking requirement only | 🟡 Medium | Oral disclosures may not be protected |
| No written confirmation for oral disclosures | 🟢 Low | Creates ambiguity but manageable |
| Includes publicly available info | 🔴 High | Unenforceable, unreasonable |
| Vague categories ("business information") | 🟡 Medium | Creates uncertainty about what's covered |

### Standard Exclusions Checklist

Verify these exclusions are present:

- [ ] Information already public through no fault of recipient
- [ ] Information already known to recipient prior to disclosure
- [ ] Information independently developed without use of confidential info
- [ ] Information received from third party without breach
- [ ] Information required to be disclosed by law/court order

### Recommended Language

**Good Definition Pattern:**
> "Confidential Information means any information disclosed by the Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party, either directly or indirectly, in writing, orally, or by inspection of tangible objects, which is designated as 'Confidential,' 'Proprietary,' or some similar designation..."

**Required Exclusions Pattern:**
> "Confidential Information shall not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party; (b) was already known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure; (c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party; or (d) is received from a third party without breach of any obligation."

### Red Flags

1. **"All information is confidential"** - Too broad, likely unenforceable
2. **No carve-outs for public/prior knowledge** - Creates unreasonable obligations
3. **Reverse marking requirement** - "Anything not marked as non-confidential is confidential"
4. **Includes recipient's pre-existing information** - Overreaching
