# Duration and Exceptions Analysis

## Duration Clauses

### Term of Agreement vs. Confidentiality Period

Distinguish between:
- **Agreement Term**: How long the NDA is in effect
- **Confidentiality Period**: How long the obligation to protect information lasts

### Risk Assessment

| Duration Type | Typical Standard | Risk Level |
|---------------|------------------|------------|
| 1-2 years | Short-term engagements | 🟢 Low |
| 3-5 years | Standard commercial NDAs | 🟢 Low |
| 5-10 years | Sensitive information | 🟡 Medium |
| Perpetual | Trade secrets only | 🔴 High if applied broadly |
| "Reasonable period" | Vague | 🟡 Medium |

### Key Questions

1. Is there a defined end date for the agreement?
2. Does the confidentiality obligation survive termination?
3. For how long after termination?
4. Is there a distinction for trade secrets (perpetual) vs. regular confidential info?

### Red Flags

| Issue | Severity | Concern |
|-------|----------|---------|
| No defined term | 🔴 High | Uncertainty, potential perpetual obligation |
| Perpetual for all information | 🔴 High | Unreasonable burden, may be unenforceable |
| Survival clause > 10 years | 🟡 Medium | May be excessive for non-trade-secret info |
| No survival clause | 🟡 Medium | Obligation ends with agreement - risky for discloser |

## Standard Exceptions

### Mandatory Exceptions (Should Always Be Present)

1. **Public Information**
   > Information that becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party

2. **Prior Knowledge**
   > Information the Receiving Party already knew before disclosure

3. **Independent Development**
   > Information independently developed without using the Disclosing Party's information

4. **Third-Party Source**
   > Information received from a third party without breach of confidentiality

5. **Legal Compulsion**
   > Information required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order

### Legal Compulsion - Best Practice Language

> "If the Receiving Party is required by law to disclose Confidential Information, it shall provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of such requirement, so that the Disclosing Party may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy."

### Risk Assessment for Missing Exceptions

| Missing Exception | Severity | Impact |
|-------------------|----------|--------|
| Public information | 🔴 High | Creates unreasonable obligation |
| Prior knowledge | 🔴 High | May prevent using own knowledge |
| Independent development | 🟡 Medium | Stifles innovation |
| Third-party source | 🟡 Medium | Unrealistic burden |
| Legal compulsion | 🔴 High | Creates legal conflict |

## Survival Clause Analysis

### Standard Survival Patterns

**Short-form:**
> "The obligations under this Agreement shall survive for a period of [X] years after termination."

**Trade-secret specific:**
> "For trade secrets, the confidentiality obligations shall survive indefinitely."

### Recommended Redline for Excessive Survival

**Original:** "The obligations under this Agreement shall survive in perpetuity."

**Redline:** "The obligations under this Agreement shall survive for a period of three (3) years after termination, except for trade secrets, which shall survive indefinitely."
